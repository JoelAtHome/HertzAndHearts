Electrode Placement

Place and verify all three channels before powering on the EX4.

⚠
Clean all skin contact surfaces with an alcohol wipe before applying electrodes. Ensure 100% surface contact to prevent edge-loading burns.

CH 1  taVNS — Left Ear + Mastoid

1 Cathode (−)

Place on the Cymba Conchae (recessed valley above the ear canal) or clip to the inner Tragus. Apply conductive electrode gel.

2 Anode (+)

Place 1–2" patch on the Mastoid Process (bony bump behind the ear). If facial twitching occurs, move further back.

  • CH 1 electrodes placed and secure

CH 2  MNS — Right Wrist

1 Cathode (−)

Place on the inner (volar) wrist, 2–3 inches from the wrist crease, between the two prominent tendons.

2 Anode (+)

Place 3–5 cm further up the arm toward the inner elbow, following the line of the median nerve.

  • CH 2 electrodes placed and secure

CH 3  TNS — Forehead

1 Cathode (−)

Place 2 cm above the midpoint of the eyebrow (supraorbital nerve exit point).

2 Anode (+)

Place higher on the forehead near the hairline, or laterally toward the temple on the same side.

  • CH 3 electrodes placed and secure

5-Minute Start Sequence

Ramp up channels one at a time. Monitor for autonomic startle responses between each addition.

⚠
Vagal Escape Rule: If HR increases by >15 BPM after adding any channel, reduce that channel's intensity by 50%. Terminate if HR does not stabilize within 60 seconds.
0:00 — Start CH 1 (taVNS)

Begin Channel 1. Slowly increase intensity until a "tingle" is felt, then back off slightly. This establishes the Vagal Ground — the safety base layer.

2:00 — Add CH 2 (MNS)

Start Channel 2. Slowly increase intensity and look for a slight thumb twitch. If fingers curl into a fist, intensity is too high. This establishes the Motor Anchor.

4:00 — Add CH 3 (TNS)

Start Channel 3. Increase to perception threshold only. Watch for rapid blinking, grimacing, or jaw clenching. This establishes the Cortical Reset.

5:00 — Full Stack Active

All three channels running. Confirm no adverse responses. Proceed to active monitoring.
