Treatment Modality Selection

Select the stimulation configuration based on the patient's current clinical presentation.

Electrode Placement

Place and verify electrodes for the selected channels before powering on the EX4.

⚠
Clean all skin contact surfaces with an alcohol wipe before applying electrodes. Ensure 100% surface contact to prevent edge-loading burns.

CH 1  taVNS — Left Ear + Mastoid

1 Cathode (−)

Place on the Cymba Conchae (recessed valley above the ear canal) using an adhesive patch electrode, or clip to the inner Tragus with conductive electrode gel. A custom ear mold with gel may also be used.

2 Anode (+)

Place a 2.5–5 cm patch on the Mastoid Process (bony bump behind the ear). If facial twitching occurs after the channel is activated, this electrode should be moved further back.

  • CH 1 electrodes placed and secure

CH 2  MNS — Right Wrist

1 Cathode (−)

Place on the inner (volar) wrist, 5–7 cm from the wrist crease, between the two prominent tendons.

2 Anode (+)

Place 3–5 cm further up the arm toward the inner elbow, following the line of the median nerve.

  • CH 2 electrodes placed and secure

CH 3  TNS — Forehead

1 Cathode (−)

Place 2 cm above the midpoint of the eyebrow (supraorbital nerve exit point).

2 Anode (+)

Place higher on the forehead near the hairline, or laterally toward the temple on the same side.

  • CH 3 electrodes placed and secure

Start Sequence

Ramp up channels one at a time. Monitor for autonomic startle responses between each addition.

⚠
Vagal Escape Rule: If HR increases by >15 BPM after adding any channel, reduce that channel's intensity by 50%. HR will be monitored automatically for 60 seconds. If HR does not stabilize, a termination prompt will appear.

Demo: Vagal Escape Popup
0:00 — Start CH 1 (taVNS)

Begin Channel 1. Slowly increase intensity until just below perception threshold, typically 1–5 mA. This establishes the Vagal Ground — the safety base layer.

2:00 — Add CH 2 (MNS)

Start Channel 2. Slowly increase intensity and look for a slight thumb twitch. If fingers curl into a fist, intensity is too high. This establishes the Motor Anchor.

4:00 — Add CH 3 (TNS)

Start Channel 3. Increase to perception threshold only. Watch for rapid blinking, grimacing, or jaw clenching — these indicate a trigeminal-autonomic reflex. This establishes the Cortical Reset.

5:00 — All Channels Active

All selected channels running. Confirm no adverse responses. Proceed to active monitoring.

Abort the session. Press Stop/Pause on the EX4 interface immediately.
